1. Scope and ministry identity

This Policy applies to the website at jesuschristsavestoken.com and JCS pages that link to it, including the home portal, Vision & Core Values page, Sign the Ledger prayer-NFT and testimony interface, wallet connection tools, live XRP Ledger metrics, Scripture and media features, and project support links.

“JCS,” “Jesus Christ Saves Token,” “the ministry,” “we,” “us,” and “our” refer to the faith-based project responsible for those pages. “User,” “you,” and “your” refer to a visitor, wallet holder, testimony author, NFT minter, reader, listener, or person interacting with the site.

The ministry’s religious purpose is to bear witness to Jesus Christ, provide access to Christian teaching, preserve prayer and testimony in durable digital forms, and support voluntary Christian fellowship and outreach. Technology is a tool of the ministry; it is not salvation, a sacrament, a church membership record, or a substitute for Scripture, prayer, fellowship, pastoral care, or professional advice.

2. Information involved

Public wallet and ledger information

A connected XRP Ledger address, transaction hash, ledger index, transaction type, NFT identifier, trustline, token balance, offer, AMM interaction, memo, and other validated XRPL data may be read or displayed. This information already exists on a public blockchain.

Information you deliberately submit

A prayer, testimony, praise report, prayer request, Scripture reference, optional display name, optional handle, reply, Amen reaction, visibility selection, or other content may be processed when you choose to create or publish it.

Local device information

The browser may store a draft, theme choice, audio volume, listening preference, incomplete publication state, or similar settings in local storage on your device. These items are normally not transmitted to JCS merely because they are stored locally.

Technical and hosting information

The hosting provider, network provider, security service, or browser may automatically process an IP address, user agent, date and time, requested page, referring page, error data, or security event. JCS does not promise that infrastructure providers keep no logs.

Communications

When you email the ministry or contact the project through X or another platform, the message, account identifier, address, attachments, and response history may be retained as reasonably necessary to answer, document, or protect the ministry.

No wallet secrets

JCS does not need and should never request your Xaman secret, family seed, recovery phrase, private key, device passcode, or other wallet credential. Do not provide those items to JCS or to anyone claiming to represent the ministry.

3. Religious and sensitive information

Prayer, testimony, Scripture references, and participation in a Christian ministry may reveal or strongly suggest a person’s religious beliefs. In some jurisdictions, information revealing religious or philosophical beliefs receives heightened legal protection.

Do not publish when disclosure could place you or another person in danger. This is especially important for people facing religious persecution, governmental monitoring, family violence, employment retaliation, social exclusion, or restrictions on Christian worship. Use no real name, location, employer, school, congregation, handle, or identifying detail when those facts could create risk.

By deliberately signing and publishing a prayer, testimony, reply, Amen reaction, or prayer NFT, you instruct the wallet and XRP Ledger network to process the information contained in the transaction. A public-ledger publication is not private, confidential, anonymous, or erasable. A shortened or hidden wallet display on the JCS interface changes only the presentation on that page; it does not remove the originating wallet address from XRPL.

4. How information is used

Information may be processed for the following ministry and operational purposes:

  • Connect a wallet through Xaman and display the connected public account.
  • Prepare user-requested XRPL transactions for review and voluntary signing.
  • Mint prayer NFTs, publish testimony records, display replies, and count Amen reactions.
  • Reconstruct the public testimony feed from validated XRP Ledger history.
  • Display live DEX, AMM, trustline, supply, ledger, and issuer information.
  • Preserve a local draft or incomplete signing flow on the user’s device.
  • Provide Scripture, religious education, media, project information, and ministry outreach.
  • Answer support, security, legal, or ministry communications.
  • Prevent fraud, impersonation, abuse, malicious code, spam, and unauthorized access.
  • Comply with lawful obligations and defend the rights, safety, mission, and property of JCS and others.
  • Improve accessibility, reliability, mobile usability, and the clarity of warnings and disclosures.

Where applicable law requires a legal basis, processing may rely on your deliberate instruction or consent, performance of a requested service, the ministry’s legitimate interest in operating and securing the site, compliance with law, protection of vital interests, or other lawful bases available to religious and nonprofit activity. Public XRPL data may also be processed because it is manifestly public and necessary to provide the ledger-facing service you requested.

5. XRP Ledger permanence and public visibility

The XRP Ledger is a decentralized public network. A validated transaction can be copied, indexed, displayed, archived, analyzed, or redistributed by independent nodes, explorers, wallets, applications, researchers, governments, and other persons outside JCS control.

  • JCS cannot privately erase a validated XRPL transaction.
  • Removing a post from the JCS interface would not remove it from XRPL.
  • A wallet address is a persistent public identifier and may be linked with other activity.
  • Transaction memos may reveal the testimony, Scripture reference, name, handle, or other content submitted.
  • NFT metadata and transaction references may remain available after this website changes or closes.

Before signing, review every field in Xaman, including network, transaction type, destination, amount, fee, issuer, currency, NFT data, memo, and security challenge. Decline any request that does not match what you intended.

6. Cookies and local browser storage

JCS pages may use browser local storage or similar device storage for functional settings such as theme, volume, draft text, a pending NFT publication, or a temporary application state. JCS does not presently use those settings to build an advertising profile or sell behavioral data.

You can clear local data through your browser settings. Clearing it may remove an unfinished draft, pending publication reference, saved display preference, or audio setting. It does not erase transactions already validated on XRPL.

Third-party services opened or embedded by the site may use their own cookies, storage, tokens, or tracking technologies under their own policies.

Hosting and security providers

Links to third parties are provided for convenience, independent verification, or requested functionality. Their terms and policies govern their services.

8. Sharing, advertising, and sale of information

JCS does not intend to sell personal information, rent a mailing list, or provide testimony data to advertisers for cross-context behavioral advertising. JCS may disclose information:

  • At your direction, including when you sign a public transaction or choose a social-sharing platform.
  • To technical providers necessary to host, secure, or operate the site.
  • When information is already public on XRPL or another public service.
  • To investigate fraud, impersonation, security threats, abuse, or harm.
  • When reasonably believed necessary to comply with valid law, legal process, or governmental demand.
  • To protect the rights, safety, religious mission, property, and lawful interests of JCS or others.
  • As part of an organizational restructuring, succession, transfer, or preservation of ministry assets, subject to applicable law.

Because JCS does not intend to sell or share personal information for targeted advertising, a “Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information” transaction is not presently necessary for that purpose. A legally valid privacy signal will not be used to override a user’s own deliberate instruction to publish a transaction on XRPL.

9. Retention and deletion

JCS retains direct communications, security records, operational records, and support history only as reasonably necessary for the purpose collected, ministry administration, dispute prevention, legal compliance, or protection of the project and its users.

Local browser data remains until you clear it, the page clears it, or the browser removes it. Third-party providers determine their own retention periods. Public XRPL records may persist indefinitely and cannot be deleted by JCS.

A request to remove information from a JCS-controlled page will be evaluated where technically and legally possible, but removal from the interface does not remove independent copies, search results, screenshots, archives, explorer records, or the underlying XRP Ledger entry.

Deletion

11. Children and minors

The site contains general Christian content but is not designed to knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. A child under 13 should not connect a wallet, mint an NFT, publish a testimony, submit a name or handle, or contact JCS with personal information without a parent or legal guardian’s involvement and any legally required consent.

Minors should not publish their age, school, location, congregation, family information, photograph, legal name, wallet ownership details, or any fact that could create a safety risk. Parents and guardians should understand that public XRPL activity is permanent and visible.

A parent or guardian who believes a child submitted off-ledger information directly to JCS may contact the ministry for review. JCS cannot delete a validated public blockchain transaction.

12. Security and wallet safety

JCS uses reasonable technical and organizational measures appropriate to the project’s size and functions, but no website, browser, wallet, network, email system, or blockchain interaction is guaranteed secure.

  • Use the official domain and independently verify the JCS issuer.
  • Review the Xaman security challenge and every transaction field.
  • Never provide a seed, recovery phrase, private key, or device code.
  • Use a device, browser, and wallet version that receive security updates.
  • Avoid public testimony that reveals dangerous personal information.
  • Report suspected impersonation, malicious requests, or security defects promptly.

A security incident may be investigated, contained, documented, and disclosed when required by applicable law or reasonably necessary to protect users.

13. International access and persecuted believers

JCS is accessible across jurisdictions with different privacy, religious-freedom, financial, blockchain, and content laws. You are responsible for considering whether access, wallet use, public Christian expression, token activity, or publication could expose you to legal or personal risk where you live.

People in environments hostile to Christianity should prioritize physical safety. Reading written content without connecting a wallet may be safer than creating an on-ledger record. A VPN, private browser, hidden-wallet display, alias, or shortened address does not guarantee anonymity.

Information may be processed in the United States or another country where a service provider, node, social platform, or infrastructure operator is located. Those locations may provide different legal protections.

14. Ministry communications and confidentiality

A message sent through the website, email, social media, wallet memo, testimony feed, or NFT is not automatically a confidential pastoral communication, confession, counseling record, attorney-client communication, medical record, or communication protected by clergy-penitent privilege.

Do not send information requiring secrecy through a public transaction or ordinary email. JCS does not promise pastoral counseling, crisis intervention, sacramental care, emergency response, or clergy confidentiality through the website.

A public prayer request may be read by anyone. When praying for another person, do not disclose their private medical, family, legal, financial, location, or identity information without lawful authority and their informed permission.

15. Changes to this Policy

JCS may revise this Policy to reflect changes in the ministry, website, technology, law, third-party services, security practices, or XRP Ledger functions. The effective date and version will be updated when material changes are published.

Continued use after an update means the revised Policy applies to later use. A policy change cannot make a previously validated blockchain transaction private or erasable.
