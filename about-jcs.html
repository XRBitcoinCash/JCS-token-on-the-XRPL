Vision & Core Values

This is a living statement of intent and responsibility—not a technical white paper, a securities offering, or a promise of financial return.

Living mission statement

The mission is Christ; the technology is a tool. JCS exists to carry testimony about Jesus, preserve prayer and Scripture references in durable digital form, and invite people into a public record of faith without surrendering custody of their wallets or relying on one organization to hold their artifacts.

A prayer NFT can serve as a portable Christian artifact inside a person’s wallet: a small, verifiable object connected to a prayer, testimony, Scripture reference, and public transaction. For someone living where Christian materials are restricted or where open worship can result in harassment, exclusion, or persecution, a wallet-held artifact may provide another way to retain and share a witness. It is not invisible and it is not automatically anonymous; XRP Ledger transactions and wallet addresses are public. The project must therefore place safety, informed choice, and honest disclosure ahead of growth.

The wider vision is to spread the Gospel and the words of Jesus across lands, languages, generations, and circumstances. JCS should make Christian content easier to encounter, preserve, verify, and carry while remaining clear that salvation, discipleship, and spiritual life do not come from a token or an NFT.

Christ centered

Every feature should direct attention toward Jesus Christ rather than toward speculation, personalities, or technological novelty.

Safety and human dignity

People facing persecution must be able to read silently, avoid publishing identity details, and understand that public-ledger activity can be traced.

Non-custodial participation

Users review and sign through Xaman. The site should never request a secret, recovery phrase, or private key.

Transparent infrastructure

Issuer identity, transactions, order books, AMM liquidity, and public records should be independently inspectable on XRPL.

Voluntary witness

No prayer, testimony, NFT, trade, or social action should occur without a deliberate user choice and wallet approval.

Stewardship over hype

JCS is not a promise of profit. Utility, accessibility, responsible disclosure, and faithful service take priority over promotional claims.

Serving people where faith carries risk

The project’s ministry purpose includes people who may be oppressed, monitored, isolated, or persecuted for Christian belief.

Safety before publicity

Portable Christian artifacts

Prayer NFTs can place a durable Christian artifact in the user’s own wallet, connected to a testimony or Scripture reference that can persist independently of a single website.

Silent access

The written vision, Scripture, prayer templates, and ledger records must remain useful without sound. Media should never be the only path to the message.

Identity restraint

Users should avoid names, locations, handles, or personal details when disclosure could create danger. Hiding a wallet in the interface does not remove it from the public ledger.

Across languages and generations

The long-term direction includes accessible translations, varied prayer references, and durable formats that can carry the Gospel beyond one region, platform, or moment in time.
