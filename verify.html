1 · Mint the NFT

Xaman signs an NFTokenMint in your wallet. The immutable URI points to the official JCS logo, while the complete prayer and its SHA-256 commitment are included in the signed mint transaction memos.

2 · Assign the prayer ID

Once the mint validates, the page uses its immutable ledger index and transaction position to create an identifier such as Prayer 1/L105782761-T004. No full-history scan or central counter is required.

3 · Publish the social record

A one-drop registry transaction links the prayer ID, NFTokenID, mint transaction, testimony, optional identity fields, Scripture reference, and content commitment into the public feed.

4 · Read from the ledger

Recent testimonies, replies, and Amen reactions are read from validated collector-account history through multiple public XRPL servers. Local storage is never the public feed database.
