1 · Reserve the prayer number

A one-drop payment creates a validated reservation on the JCS collector account. Reservations are ordered by ledger index and transaction index, producing Prayer 1/00000001, Prayer 1/00000002, and so on.

2 · Mint the numbered NFT

Your wallet mints an NFToken whose immutable data URI contains compact JSON metadata: the assigned prayer number, a description, and the official JCS logo URL. The complete testimony is preserved in the signed mint memos.

3 · Publish to the social ledger

A second one-drop registry payment links the reservation, NFT ID, mint transaction, testimony, author fields, Scripture reference, and SHA-256 commitment into the shared feed.

4 · Read directly from XRPL

The rolling feed is rebuilt from validated collector-account history using multiple public XRPL WebSocket servers. Local storage is used only for an unfinished draft or recovery between required signatures.
